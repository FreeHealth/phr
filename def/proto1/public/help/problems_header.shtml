Medical Conditions

A medical condition is a medical problem that needs to be treated or managed. It can be a diagnosis, syndrome, or symptom.

What do I need to know about the medical conditions list?

  • Record all current medical conditions as well as serious conditions you have had in the past. As you type, the system will suggest medical conditions to you.
  • Record all serious short-term conditions (e.g., a fracture, diabetes during pregnancy), as well as a major symptom if the cause of the symptom is unknown (e.g., fatigue, shortness of breath).
  • If you do not find your specific medical condition in the selection list, simply type the name as you know it.
  • The PHR system will provide you with an button next to many of the medical conditions you list. Click this button for additional reliable consumer health information from the National Institutes of Health and other trusted sources.
  • You can also find the latest medical research studies related to particular medical diseases or conditions by clicking on the Research Studies button at the top of the Medical Conditions section.

What kind of information should I enter?

All fields highlighted with are required fields.
Medical Condition The name of the medical condition
Status Select Active for conditions currently present or being treated; Inactive for cured or not currently present conditions
Started The date the condition began
Stopped The date when the condition ended or was resolved
Description/Comment Any additional information about your medical condition

Why is this important?

Both present and past medical conditions are relevant to your overall health because they can provide your doctor with important clues about your current symptoms and the need for follow-up.
