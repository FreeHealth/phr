Allergies and Other Dangerous Reactions

An allergy is a negative response by your immune system to something (e.g., cat hair, pollen, bee sting) that does not bother most other people. Allergies can cause mild reactions, including runny nose, sneezing, itching, and rash, or severe ones, such as difficulty breathing and low blood pressure (i.e., anaphylactic shock).

What do I need to know about the PHR allergies list?

  • Many medications may cause mild side effects, such as nausea, constipation, sleepiness, or dry mouth. These are effects of the medication - not allergies - and should not be listed here.
  • If you select Medications Specific as the allergy type, the PHR will display a list of brand name drugs in capital letters along with the generic drug name displayed in mixed case next to each medication.
  • If you do not find the specific allergy you have in the selection list, simply type the name of the allergy as you know it.
  • The PHR system will provide you with an button next to many of the allergies you list. Click this link for additional reliable consumer health information from the National Institutes of Health and other trusted sources.

What kind of information should I enter?

All fields highlighted with are required fields.

TypeIndicate the type of allergy you have (e.g. medicine, food, other)
NameEnter the name of the substance (i.e. allergen) that causes your allergy
ReactionDescribe the physical reaction caused by the substance
StartedThe date when you or the patient first experienced symptoms of an allergic or dangerous reaction to the substance.
CommentsAdditional information or comments you have regarding the allergic reaction.

Why is this important?

A complete list of your known allergies will provide your doctor with important information concerning treatment options and the need for follow-up.
