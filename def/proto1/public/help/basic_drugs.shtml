Medications

Medications include all of the prescription medications as well as over-the-counter (non-prescription) medications, vitamins, nutritional supplements and herbal products that you take.

What do I need to know about the PHR medications list?

  • Medication names displayed in capital letters are brand name drugs and drug names displayed in mixed case are generic drug names. You may select either type of name.
  • When searching for a medication, you may notice that the same medication name with different dosage forms may be displayed, for example penicillin (Oral-pill) and penicillin (Injectable). Be sure to pay attention to these distinctions. Penicillin (Oral-pill) refers to a pill taken by mouth, whereas penicillin (Injectable) refers to penicillin given by a shot or through an IV.
  • If you do not find the specific medication you are using in the selection list, simply type the name and form of the medication as you know it.
  • The PHR system will provide you with an Info Page link next to many of the medications you list. Click this link for additional reliable consumer health information from the National Institutes of Health and other trusted sources.
  • The PHR system displays a list of all medications labeled as Active followed by all medicationss labeled as Stopped.

What kind of information should I enter?

All fields highlighted with are required fields.

Medication nameThe name of all medications you are taking along with the correct dosage form. If you have been prescribed medications that you are not currently taking, enter them as well.
StatusActive status means that you are currently taking the medication. If you have entered a medication that you have taken in the past, and are no longer taking, mark the status as Stopped.
StrengthThe strength and form (also known as the formulation) of the medication (e.g., 325 mg caps).
InstructionsInstructions that your doctor or pharmacist has provided regarding this medication (e.g., take with food).
StartedThe date you or the patient started taking the medication.
StoppedThe date you or the patient stopped taking the medication.
Why StoppedFor a medication you are no longer taking, select the reason that you stopped taking it or enter your own.
ResupplyThe date you or the patient will need a new supply of medication.

Why is this important?

A complete list of both your prescription and non-prescription medications will provide your doctor with information about your current symptoms, possible drug interactions, and the need for follow-up.
