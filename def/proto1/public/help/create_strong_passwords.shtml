Creating Strong Passwords

When creating your password, please keep these tips in mind to help protect your account:

  1. Use a combination of at least three of the following types of characters:
    • Capital letters
    • Lowercase letters
    • Numeric characters
    • Special characters (!@#$%^&*()_+|~-=\`{}[]:";'<>?,./)
  2. Choose a password that has at least eight characters. The longer a password is, the harder it may be for hackers to guess, although choosing a string of words commonly found together is not a good strategy. For example, the password "star2H@nd" is a better password than "northstar".
  3. Change your password often. Security experts recommend changing your passwords at least every 60 days. Choose a password that is different from your previous passwords.
  4. Choose passwords you can easily remember or store them in a safe place away from your computer.
  5. Avoid using
    • Dictionary words in any language.
    • Words or numbers associated with your personal information, such as names of loved ones or pets, social security numbers, or birthdays.
    • Repeated or a sequence of numbers or letters such as "111111" or "abcdefg".
    • The same password for all of your accounts.
